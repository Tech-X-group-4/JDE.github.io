DOES RAM AFFECT HDD AND SSD?

Yes and no, while they are both considered storage devices and store data and programs, RAM is used to store computer programs and data that the CPU needs in real-time and RAM data is volatile and is erased once the computer is switched off. HDD and SSD is a permanent storage device that stores data permanently even after the computer is turned off and it is used to store user-specific data and operating files. However, if your device has lower than the minimum amount of RAM needed, then you’ll start seeing problems with your read and write speed because while they are 2 separate devices, HDD and SSD still need their minimum requirement of RAM to function properly since RAM acts as a buffer between the 2 drives. The transferring of data usually goes to the RAM first and then to the HDD and/or SSD.
